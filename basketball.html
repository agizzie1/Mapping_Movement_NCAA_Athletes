NCAA Division 1 Basketball Transfer Movement

This tool was created to depict the movement of NCAA D1 Men's Basketball student-athletes in the context of an ever-evolving college NIL landscape. With athletes moving between schools and conferences in search of higher paying deals and no cap on the number of times a player can enter the NCAA transfer portal, tracking student-athlete movement is essential to understanding the impact of NIL since July 2025 on student-athlete experiences.

This tool will display movement within and between conferences and schools. It can be used to visualize player movement, sort by position, date, grade transferred, number of times transferred, and more.

Context

The July 2025- July 2026 window of the Mapping Movement tool reflects the NIL landscape shift in NCAA sports that occurred in June of 2025. In a landmark Supreme Court decision in June 2025, House vs. NCAA, the court ruled that athletes could now take home a share of the revenue that their schools generate each year from broadcasting competitions. As of its enactment on July 1st 2025, the NIL landscape forever changed. Athletes entering the transfer portal could now be more directly fueled by university promises of paychecks, revenue sharing, NIL collective money, brand partnerships, more social media presence, and more. As of January 2026, the NCAA changed the transfer portal rules for Men’s and Women’s D1 basketball to a 15-day period opening the day after the NCAA championship game concludes. The new rule went into effect immediately on January 14th 2026. For changes in coaching staff, a 15-day transfer window period will open 5 days after a new coach is hired or announced. If no new coach is hired within 30 days after the prior head coach departs the school, on the 31st day a new 15-day transfer window will open as well. Click here for more information about NCAA eligibility.

Another change that will affect this coming year’s transfer portal is the NCAA’s new eligibility model. Finalized in June of 2026, NCAA D1 eligibility rules have changed to a 5-in-5 model. The rule stipulates that athletes now have 5 seasons of eligibility to play their sport in the NCAA, provided that they enroll in university no later than the academic year after their 19th birthday. The eligibility clock starts either when A) an athlete enrolls in university or B) when an athlete turns 19 years old. For the NCAA, this eliminates complicated Red Shirt waivers and eligibility extension requests for injury. Now, fully implemented for the class of athletes that will enroll in the fall of 2027, athletes will have 5 years of eligibility with no option for redshirt or eligibility extensions (besides some exceptions including religious missions, pregnancy, or active duty military service). If no changes are made to rules allowing athletes to enter the transfer portal as many times as they want, this 5-in-5 eligibility rule will enable athletes to transfer up to 4 times in their careers to play 5 seasons for 5 different universities.
