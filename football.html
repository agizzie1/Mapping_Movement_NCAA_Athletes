NCAA Division 1 Football Transfer Movement

This tool was created to depict the movement of NCAA D1 Football student-athletes in the context of an ever-evolving college NIL landscape. With athletes moving between schools and conferences in search of higher paying deals and no cap on the number of times a player can enter the NCAA transfer portal, tracking student-athlete movement is essential to understanding the impact of NIL since July 2025 on student-athlete experiences.

This tool displays movement within and between conferences, schools, Football Bowl Subdivision (FBS), and Football Championship Subdivision (FCS). This interactive tool can be used to visualize player movement in and out of these categories, sort by position, date, grade transferred, number of times transferred, and more.

Context

The July 2025–July 2026 window of the Mapping Movement tool reflects the NIL landscape shift in NCAA sports that occurred in June of 2025. In a landmark Supreme Court decision in June 2025, House vs. NCAA, the court ruled that athletes could now take home a share of the revenue that their schools generate each year from broadcasting competitions. As of its enactment on July 1st 2025, the NIL landscape forever changed. Athletes entering the transfer portal could now be more directly fueled by university promises of paychecks, revenue sharing, NIL collective money, brand partnerships, more social media presence, and more.

In the fall of 2025, the NCAA changed transfer portal window rules for D1 Football to eliminate the 15-day Spring transfer portal.

This limits opportunities for athletes to enter the portal and transfer schools to one 2-week Winter window from January 2–16th. Players must enroll at their new school by the January 16th entry date. For players that compete in the playoffs later in January, their opportunity to enter the transfer portal lasts for 5 days after their season ends. For coaching changes, the NCAA enacted a new rule that allows players to enter the transfer portal for a 15-day period that begins 5 days after a new head coach is hired or a head coach is fired.

This is a departure from the previous rules that offered D1 football players two windows to transfer schools: one for 30 days beginning in December, and one shorter 15-day period in the Spring. Now, players have only the new Winter window to enter the portal and enroll in a new university.

Another change that will affect this coming year’s transfer portal is the NCAA’s new eligibility model. Finalized in June of 2026, NCAA D1 eligibility rules have changed to a 5-in-5 model. The rule stipulates that athletes now have 5 seasons of eligibility to play their sport in the NCAA, provided that they enroll in university no later than the academic year after their 19th birthday. The eligibility clock starts either when A) an athlete enrolls in university or B) when an athlete turns 19 years old. For the NCAA, this eliminates complicated Red Shirt waivers and eligibility extension requests for injury. Now, fully implemented for the class of athletes that will enroll in the fall of 2027, athletes will have 5 years of eligibility with no option for redshirt or eligibility extensions (besides some exceptions including religious missions, pregnancy, or active duty military service). If no changes are made to rules allowing athletes to enter the transfer portal as many times as they want, this 5-in-5 eligibility rule will enable athletes to transfer up to 4 times in their careers to play 5 seasons for 5 different universities.
